Electric Charges & Fields

God Master Notes. Logic & Derivations Unlocked.

Essential Constants:
• Charge of electron ($e$): $-1.6 \times 10^{-19} C$
• Mass of electron ($m_e$): $9.1 \times 10^{-31} kg$
• Mass of proton ($m_p$): $1.67 \times 10^{-27} kg$
• Permittivity of space ($\epsilon_0$): $8.854 \times 10^{-12} C^2/Nm^2$
• Coulomb Constant ($k$): $9 \times 10^9 Nm^2/C^2$

1. Electric Charge

DEFINITION Electric Charge is the intrinsic property that creates electric force.
Kid's Explanation Think of charge as a "Social Personality".
  • Protons (+) and Electrons (-) are opposites.
  • Rule: Opposites attract (Best friends), Likes repel (Enemies).

1.1 Basic Properties

  • Additivity: Total Q = sum of all q's.
  • Conservation: Charge is never lost, only moved.
  • Quantization ($q = ne$): Charge comes in packets of $e$.
Solved Example: How many electrons in 1C?

Q: Find n for q = 1C.

Logic: Use $n = q/e$.

Calc: $1 / (1.6 \times 10^{-19}) = 6.25 \times 10^{18}$ electrons.

2. Coulomb's Law

LAW $F = k \frac{|q_1 q_2|}{r^2}$
Why Inverse Square? ($1/r^2$) As distance doubles, the influence spreads out over an area 4 times larger (Area of sphere = $4\pi r^2$). So the strength dilutes by 4.

3. Electric Field

DEFINITION Force per unit positive test charge. $\vec{E} = \vec{F}/q_0$.
Why must the Test Charge be small?

We use $\lim_{q_0 \to 0}$. If $q_0$ were large, its own strong field would push the source charge away, altering the very thing we are trying to measure! It's like checking the temperature of a water drop with a giant frozen thermometer.

Why E-Field is Normal to Conductors? If field lines were tilted, there would be a component along the surface ($E \cos\theta$). This force would push free electrons, causing a current to flow. Since static means "no flow", the field MUST be perpendicular.

4. Electric Dipole

System of equal and opposite charges separated by $2a$.

4.1 Torque on Dipole (The Mechanism)

$\vec{\tau} = \vec{p} \times \vec{E} = pE \sin\theta$
The "How & Why" of Torque

1. Why is Net Force Zero?
The positive charge feels force $+qE$ (Right). The negative feels $-qE$ (Left). Since they are equal and opposite, they cancel out linearly. The dipole doesn't move forward or backward.

2. Then Why does it Rotate?
Imagine a steering wheel. You pull down with one hand and push up with the other. Net force is zero, but since the forces act at different points (different lines of action), they create a twist. This is called a Couple.

3. Why $\sin\theta$?
Torque depends on the perpendicular distance between forces ($2a \sin\theta$).
$\tau = \text{Force} \times \text{Perpendicular Dist} = (qE) \times (2a \sin\theta) = pE \sin\theta$.

State Angle Torque Why does it behave this way?
Stable 0° 0 Dipole is aligned. If you disturb it, a "restoring torque" pulls it back.
Max Torque 90° $pE$ Lever arm is maximum. Best angle to rotate.
Unstable 180° 0 Dipole is anti-parallel. If you disturb it slightly, it flips over violently to 0°.
Derivation: Axial Field ($2kp/r^3$)
  1. $E_{net} = E_+ - E_- = kq/(r-a)^2 - kq/(r+a)^2$
  2. Combine fractions: $E = kq \frac{4ra}{(r^2-a^2)^2}$
  3. For short dipole ($r \gg a$), $a \to 0$:
  4. $E = kq \frac{4r}{r^4} = \frac{2(q \cdot 2a)k}{r^3} = \frac{2kp}{r^3}$
Derivation: Equatorial Field ($-kp/r^3$)
  1. Magnitudes equal: $E_+ = E_-$. Vertical components cancel.
  2. Horizontal add: $E_{net} = 2E \cos\theta$.
  3. $\cos\theta = a/r$. So $E = 2 \frac{kq}{r^2} \frac{a}{r} = \frac{kp}{r^3}$.
  4. Direction is opposite to dipole moment $\vec{p}$, hence negative.

5. Gauss Law

LAW $\Phi = \oint \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{A} = \frac{q_{in}}{\epsilon_0}$
Why does Shape not matter? Flux is like water flowing out of a pipe. If you put a bag over the pipe, the amount of water coming out of the bag is the same, whether the bag is small, big, round, or square. The source (charge) determines the flow, not the container (surface).

Applications (Derivations)

1. Infinite Wire ($E \propto 1/r$)

Logic: Use a Cylinder. Field is radial, so it only pierces the curved part.

  • $\Phi = E \times (2\pi rl)$
  • Charge $q = \lambda l$
  • $E(2\pi rl) = \lambda l / \epsilon_0 \Rightarrow E = \frac{\lambda}{2\pi\epsilon_0 r}$
2. Infinite Sheet (Constant E)

Logic: Use a Pillbox piercing the sheet.

  • Flux through 2 flat faces = $2EA$.
  • Charge $q = \sigma A$.
  • $2EA = \sigma A / \epsilon_0 \Rightarrow E = \frac{\sigma}{2\epsilon_0}$
3. Spherical Shell
  • Outside: Behaves as if all charge is at center ($kq/r^2$).
  • Inside: No charge enclosed ($q=0$), so Field $E=0$.
NEET/JEE Rank Booster
  • Soap Bubble: Radius INCREASES if charged (Repulsion pushes surface out).
  • Satellite Pendulum: Angle = 180°, Tension = Force between charges.
  • Cube Flux: Center ($q/\epsilon_0$), Face ($q/2\epsilon_0$), Corner ($q/8\epsilon_0$).
