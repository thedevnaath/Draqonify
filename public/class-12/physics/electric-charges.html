Electric Charges & Fields

The "God Master" Notes. Every concept, definition, and secret trick.

1. Electrostatics & Charge

DEFINITION: Electrostatics The branch of physics that deals with the study of forces, fields, and potentials arising from static charges (charges at rest).

1.1 Electric Charge

Charge is an intrinsic property of elementary particles (like mass) which gives rise to electric force.

  • Two Types: Positive (Proton) and Negative (Electron). Named by Benjamin Franklin.
  • Polarity: The property that differentiates the two kinds of charges.
  • Interaction: Like charges repel; Unlike charges attract.
Kid's Explanation Imagine Magnets. If you try to push the North pole of one magnet to the North pole of another, they fight and push back (Repel). But North and South click together instantly (Attract). Charges are just like tiny, invisible magnets!
Deep Dive: Gold Leaf Electroscope

A device used to detect the presence and polarity of charge.

  • Construction: A metal rod with a knob at top and two thin gold leaves at bottom, inside a glass jar.
  • Working: When a charged object touches the knob, charge flows down to the leaves. Since both leaves get the same charge, they repel and diverge.
  • Degree of Divergence: Measures the amount of charge.

1.2 Conductors vs Insulators

  • Conductors: Allow electricity to pass through them easily (e.g., Metals, Humans, Earth). They have free electrons.
  • Insulators: Do not allow passage of electricity (e.g., Glass, Plastic, Nylon).
Kid's Explanation Think of Conductors like a Highway where cars (electrons) can zoom freely.
Think of Insulators like a Thick Jungle; the cars get stuck and can't move anywhere.

1.3 Methods of Charging

  1. By Friction: Rubbing two insulators. Electrons are physically torn off one and added to the other.
  2. By Conduction: Direct contact between a charged body and a neutral conductor.
  3. By Induction: Charging without contact (Polarization + Grounding).
Kid's Explanation Friction: Rubbing a balloon on your hair. You are literally stealing electrons from your hair!
Conduction: Shaking hands with a zombie—now you are infected too (charge shares).
Induction: A zombie stands near you (without touching). You get scared and run to the other side of the room. The fear (charge) is induced!

1.4 Basic Properties of Charge

  • Additivity: Charges add up like numbers. ($Q = +2C - 3C + 5C = +4C$).
  • Conservation: Charge is never created or destroyed, only transferred. (Total charge of universe is constant).
  • Quantization: Charge is not continuous. It comes in packets of $e$.
    q = ne where $n$ is an integer, $e = 1.6 \times 10^{-19} C$.
Kid's Explanation Quantization: Think of buying Eggs. You can buy 1 egg, 2 eggs, or 100 eggs. But you can NEVER buy 1.5 eggs. Nature only sells electrons in whole numbers!

1.5 Continuous Charge Distribution

At macroscopic levels, quantization is ignored, and we treat charge as continuous.

  • Linear Charge Density ($\lambda$): Charge per unit length. Used for wires/rings.
    Formula: $\lambda = \frac{q}{L}$ (Unit: C/m)
  • Surface Charge Density ($\sigma$): Charge per unit area. Used for sheets/shells.
    Formula: $\sigma = \frac{q}{A}$ (Unit: C/m²)
  • Volume Charge Density ($\rho$): Charge per unit volume. Used for solid spheres.
    Formula: $\rho = \frac{q}{V}$ (Unit: C/m³)
Kid's Explanation Linear ($\lambda$): Like ants marching in a single line on a string.
Surface ($\sigma$): Like butter spread evenly over a slice of bread.
Volume ($\rho$): Like chocolate chips hidden everywhere inside a thick cookie!

2. Coulomb's Law

F = k |q₁q₂| / r²
Kid's Explanation Think of Best Friends ($q_1, q_2$). If they are close (small $r$), their bond (Force) is super strong. If they move to different cities (large $r$), the bond gets weaker very fast.

2.1 Vector Form & Superposition

Force is a vector. Net force on a charge is the vector sum of all individual forces.

Kid's Explanation If 5 people push you from different directions, you don't just add their strength ($5+5$). You have to see which direction they are pushing. Maybe two people cancel each other out!

3. Electric Field

DEFINITION: Electric Field The region around a charge where its influence is felt. Force per unit test charge.
$\vec{E} = \frac{\vec{F}}{q_0}$
Kid's Explanation Imagine a Campfire (Charge). Even if you don't touch the fire, you can feel the Heat (Field) from a distance. The closer you get, the stronger the heat (Field Strength)!

3.1 Electric Field Lines

  1. Start (+) $\to$ End (-).
  2. Never Cross.
  3. Continuous curves.
  4. Normal to conductor surface.
Kid's Explanation Think of them as Traffic Lanes. 1. Cars always drive from Plus City to Minus Town. 2. Lanes never cross (or cars would crash!). 3. Crowded lanes mean a lot of traffic (Strong Field).

4. Electric Dipole

Two equal and opposite charges separated by distance $2a$.

Kid's Explanation A Dumbbell! One side is heavy positive, one side is heavy negative. If you push the center, it doesn't move. But if you twist it, it spins!

4.1 Field Formulas

  • Axial: $E = \frac{2kp}{r^3}$ (Along axis).
  • Equatorial: $E = \frac{kp}{r^3}$ (Perpendicular bisector).

4.2 Torque on Dipole

$\vec{\tau} = pE \sin\theta$. (Tries to align dipole with field).

Kid's Explanation Think of a Compass Needle in the Earth's magnetic field. It always wants to point North. If you force it to point East, it will wiggle and fight to go back North. That "wiggle force" is Torque.

5. Gauss's Law

LAW: Gauss's Law Total Flux $\Phi = \frac{q_{enclosed}}{\epsilon_0}$
Kid's Explanation Imagine a Lightbulb inside a Paper Bag. The amount of light coming out (Flux) depends ONLY on how bright the bulb is ($q_{enclosed}$). It doesn't matter if the bag is round, square, or crumpled. The same amount of light comes out!

6. Conductors

E = 0 Inside. Charge resides on surface.

Kid's Explanation A hollow conductor is a Safe House. If there is a lightning storm (Electric Field) outside, the people inside the safe house feel absolutely nothing. The lightning flows over the roof (Surface) and goes to ground.

The Story of Electro-Kingdom

Once upon a time, there was a land called Matter. In this land lived tiny citizens called Charges. Some were Positive (Optimists) and some were Negative (Pessimists).

The Law of Interaction: When two Optimists met, they fought and pushed away (Repulsion). But when an Optimist met a Pessimist, they fell in love and pulled together (Attraction).

The Aura: Every citizen had an invisible "Vibe" around them called the Electric Field. It warned others, "I am here!" before they even touched.

The Power Couple: Sometimes, an Optimist and a Pessimist held hands and refused to let go. They became a Dipole. They wouldn't move from their spot, but if the wind (External Field) blew, they would spin and dance (Torque).

The Wizard Gauss: One day, a lazy wizard named Gauss wanted to count the citizens. Instead of counting one by one, he threw a giant Net (Surface) over a house. He just looked at the vibes (Flux) coming out of the net. He instantly knew: "Aha! There are exactly 5 citizens inside!"
