Electric Charges & Fields

God Master Notes. Concept Clarity Level: 100%.

1. Electric Charge

Electric Charge is an intrinsic property that causes force in electromagnetic fields.
ConceptifyImagine every particle has a "personality". Protons (+), Electrons (-). Opposites attract, Likes repel.

1.1 Basic Properties

  • Additivity: Total Q = sum of all q's.
  • Conservation: Charge is never lost, only moved.
  • Quantization ($q = ne$): Charge comes in packets of $e$.

2. Coulomb's Law

$$F = k \frac{|q_1 q_2|}{r^2}$$
Why Inverse Square? As distance doubles, the influence spreads over an area 4 times larger. So strength is 1/4.

3. Electric Field

Force per unit positive test charge. $\vec{E} = \vec{F}/q_0$.
Deep Dive: Why small Test Charge?
If test charge was big, it would push the source charge away!

4. Electric Dipole

Derivation: Axial Field ($2kp/r^3$)
  1. $E_{net} = E_+ - E_- = kq/(r-a)^2 - kq/(r+a)^2$
  2. Combine fractions: $E = kq \frac{4ra}{(r^2-a^2)^2}$
  3. For short dipole ($r \gg a$), $a \to 0$:
  4. $E = kq \frac{4r}{r^4} = \frac{2(q \cdot 2a)k}{r^3} = \frac{2kp}{r^3}$
Derivation: Equatorial Field ($-kp/r^3$)
  1. Magnitudes equal: $E_+ = E_-$. Vertical components cancel.
  2. Horizontal add: $E_{net} = 2E \cos\theta$.
  3. $\cos\theta = a/r$. So $E = 2 \frac{kq}{r^2} \frac{a}{r} = \frac{kp}{r^3}$.
  4. Direction is opposite to dipole moment $\vec{p}$, hence negative.

5. Gauss Law

$$\Phi = \oint \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{A} = \frac{q_{in}}{\epsilon_0}$$
1. Infinite Wire ($E \propto 1/r$)
  • Gaussian Surface: Cylinder. Flux only through curved part.
  • $\Phi = E \times (2\pi rl)$
  • Charge $q = \lambda l$
  • $E(2\pi rl) = \lambda l / \epsilon_0 \Rightarrow E = \frac{\lambda}{2\pi\epsilon_0 r}$
2. Infinite Sheet (Constant E)
  • Gaussian Surface: Pillbox. Flux through 2 flat faces.
  • $\Phi = 2EA$
  • Charge $q = \sigma A$
  • $2EA = \sigma A / \epsilon_0 \Rightarrow E = \frac{\sigma}{2\epsilon_0}$
