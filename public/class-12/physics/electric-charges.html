Electric Charges and Fields

NCERT Sequential Guide.

1.1 Introduction

The Concept: Electrostatics deals with forces, fields, and potentials arising from static charges (charges at rest).
  • Origin: The term electricity comes from the Greek word elektron (meaning amber). Thales of Miletus (600 BC) discovered that rubbing amber with wool attracts light objects.
  • Static Electricity: The electric charges generated by rubbing are static; they do not move.
Conceptify Have you ever rubbed a balloon on your hair and stuck it to a wall? That is magic... or static electricity! Rubbing wakes up the sleepy charges, and they want to stick to things.

1.2 Electric Charge

The Concept: Charge is an intrinsic property of matter (like mass) that causes it to experience a force in electric and magnetic fields.
  • Two Types: Positive and Negative.
    • Like charges repel. Unlike charges attract.
  • Gold Leaf Electroscope: Detects charge.
    • Mechanism: Charge flows to leaves -> leaves repel/diverge.
Conceptify Imagine boys are "Plus" and girls are "Minus". If two boys sit in one chair, they fight (Repel). The Gold Leaf Electroscope is a metal stick with foil legs. Give it charge, and the legs run away from each other!

1.3 Conductors and Insulators

  • Conductors: Allow flow (Copper, Earth).
  • Insulators: Resist flow (Glass, Plastic).
  • Earthing: Sharing charge with Earth to neutralize.
Conceptify Conductors are like highways (fast cars). Insulators are like swamps (cars get stuck).

1.4 Basic Properties

  1. Additivity: $Q = q_1 + q_2$.
  2. Conservation: Charge is constant in isolated system.
  3. Quantization: $q = \pm ne$.
Conceptify Quantization is like eggs. You buy 1 or 2 eggs, never 1.5 eggs.

1.6 Superposition Principle

Force on any charge is vector sum of all forces.
Conceptify If two friends pull your hands, you move in the direction of the stronger pull.

1.7 Electric Field

$$E = \frac{kQ}{r^2}$$
Conceptify A Spider (Source) makes a Web (Field). A Fly (Test Charge) feels the stickiness (Force) when it lands.

1.8 Electric Field Lines

  1. Start Pos, End Neg.
  2. No Intersection.
  3. Continuous.
  4. Perpendicular to Conductor.
Conceptify Arrows coming out of Plus (Porcupine) and going into Minus.

1.9 Electric Flux

$$\Phi = EA \cos\theta$$
Conceptify Rain passing through a ring. Flat ring = Max rain. Sideways ring = No rain.

1.10 Electric Dipole

System of equal/opposite charges.
Axial Line
$$E = 2kp/r^3$$
Equatorial Line
$$E = kp/r^3$$
Conceptify A dumbbell with +/- weights. Field at nose is double the field at side.

1.11 Dipole in Field

$$\tau = pE \sin\theta$$
Conceptify Like a compass needle twisting to line up with North.

Conceptify Light from a bulb inside a box. Amount of light depends on bulb, not box shape.

The Remember Forever Story

Spider (Charge) spins a Web (Field). A Fly (Test Charge) feels the force. To measure the spider, we put a Box (Gauss Surface) over it and count web threads (Flux).
