Electrostatic Potential and Capacitance

Scalar Side of Electricity. Energy, Work & Storage.

2.1 Electrostatic Potential ($V$)

The Concept: The amount of work done to bring a unit positive charge from Infinity to a specific point against the electric force.
$$V = \frac{W}{q_0}$$
Conceptify Imagine a hill. The ground is "Zero Potential" (Infinity). Pushing a rock (Charge) up the hill is hard work. The higher you go, the more "Potential" the rock has. If you let go, it falls back down. Potential is just "Electric Height".

2.2 Potential due to a Point Charge (Derivation üõë)

Derivation: Potential at distance r

Goal: Calculate $V$ at distance $r$ from charge $+Q$.

  1. Setup: Charge $+Q$ at Origin. Bring test charge $+1C$ from $\infty$ to point $P$ (distance $r$).
  2. Work Done ($dW$): Force is repulsive ($F = kQ/x^2$). To move it closer by $dx$, we must apply equal opposite force.
    $$dW = \vec{F}_{ext} \cdot \vec{dx} = -F dx = - \frac{kQ}{x^2} dx$$
  3. Integrate: Total work $W = \int_{\infty}^{r} dW$.
    $$W = -kQ \int_{\infty}^{r} x^{-2} dx$$
    $$W = -kQ \left[ \frac{x^{-1}}{-1} \right]_{\infty}^{r} = kQ \left[ \frac{1}{r} - \frac{1}{\infty} \right]$$
  4. Result: Since $V = W/1C$:
    $$V = \frac{kQ}{r}$$

    Note: $V \propto 1/r$ (Potential falls slower than Field $E \propto 1/r^2$).

2.3 Potential due to an Electric Dipole (Derivation üõë)

Derivation: V due to Dipole

Setup: Dipole ($-q$ at A, $+q$ at B), separation $2a$. Find $V$ at point $P$ (distance $r$, angle $\theta$).

  1. Principle: $V_{net} = V_{+q} + V_{-q}$ (Scalar addition).
    $$V = \frac{kq}{r_1} - \frac{kq}{r_2} = kq \left( \frac{1}{r_1} - \frac{1}{r_2} \right)$$
  2. Geometry Approximation: If $r \gg a$:
    $r_1 \approx r - a \cos\theta$
    $r_2 \approx r + a \cos\theta$
  3. Substitute:
    $$V = kq \left[ \frac{(r + a \cos\theta) - (r - a \cos\theta)}{r^2 - a^2 \cos^2\theta} \right]$$
    $$V = kq \frac{2a \cos\theta}{r^2}$$
  4. Result: ($p = q \times 2a$)
    $$V = \frac{kp \cos\theta}{r^2}$$
  5. Special Cases:
    • Axial ($\theta = 0^\circ$): $V = kp/r^2$ (Max).
    • Equatorial ($\theta = 90^\circ$): $V = 0$.
Conceptify On the equator of a dipole, you are equally far from the "Good Twin" (+q) and the "Evil Twin" (-q). Their pulls cancel out perfectly. The height (Potential) is zero.

2.4 Equipotential Surfaces

The Concept: A surface where the potential is the same at every point.
  1. Work done to move a charge on this surface is Zero.
  2. Electric Field is always perpendicular to the surface.
  3. They never intersect.
  4. Shapes: Point Charge (Spheres), Uniform Field (Parallel Planes).

2.5 Potential Energy of a System ($U$)

$$U = \frac{k q_1 q_2}{r_{12}}$$

Dipole in External Field:

$$U = -pE \cos\theta$$
  • Stable ($\theta=0$): $U = -pE$ (Min Energy).
  • Unstable ($\theta=180$): $U = +pE$ (Max Energy).

2.6 Electrostatics of Conductors

  1. Inside a conductor, Electrostatic Field is zero. (Shielding).
  2. At the surface, Field is normal (perpendicular).
  3. Interior is at constant Potential. ($V_{inside} = V_{surface}$).
  4. Charge resides only on the surface.
  5. Sharp Points: Surface Charge Density ($\sigma$) is higher.

2.7 Dielectrics and Polarization

  • Dielectric: A non-conducting material that gets polarized.
  • Net Field: Decreases. $E_{net} = E_0/K$.
Conceptify Imagine trying to shout through a pillow. The pillow (Dielectric) absorbs some of your sound (Field). The sound that comes out the other side is weaker.

2.8 Capacitors and Capacitance ($C$)

$$C = \frac{Q}{V}$$
Conceptify A Capacitor is a Bucket. Charge (Q) is the Water. Voltage (V) is the Water Pressure. Capacitance (C) is the width of the bucket.

2.9 The Parallel Plate Capacitor (Derivation üõë)

Derivation: Capacitance Formula

Setup: Two plates of Area $A$, separation $d$, charge density $+\sigma$ and $-\sigma$.

  1. Electric Field between plates:
    $$E = \frac{\sigma}{\epsilon_0} = \frac{Q}{A\epsilon_0}$$
  2. Potential Difference ($V = Ed$):
    $$V = \left( \frac{Q}{A\epsilon_0} \right) d$$
  3. Capacitance ($C = Q/V$):
    $$C = \frac{Q}{(Qd / A\epsilon_0)}$$
  4. Result:
    $$C = \frac{\epsilon_0 A}{d}$$

2.10 Effect of Dielectric on Capacitance

  • Field decreases ($E \to E/K$).
  • Voltage decreases ($V \to V/K$).
  • Capacitance Increases:
    $$C = \frac{K \epsilon_0 A}{d}$$
Conceptify Putting a sponge (Dielectric) between the plates makes the "Springiness" of the field weaker. This allows you to stuff MORE charge in before the voltage gets too high.

2.11 Combination of Capacitors

Series$$1/C_{eq} = 1/C_1 + 1/C_2$$
Parallel$$C_{eq} = C_1 + C_2$$

2.12 Energy Stored in a Capacitor ($U$)

  • $U = \frac{1}{2} C V^2$
  • $U = \frac{Q^2}{2C}$
  • $U = \frac{1}{2} Q V$
  • Energy Density: $u = \frac{1}{2} \epsilon_0 E^2$
